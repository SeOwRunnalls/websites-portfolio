Education

I started this course briefly after the safety measure after covid settled down. I started learning the trade when I was around 14 and quit later following some intreated health issues. After that I briefly attempted a course to become a line cook, that ended in very much the same way. After a period of recovery, I returned older and wiser. The course was more to the point there was less emphasis on drawing out schematics and more on performing the necessary steps to make something. I’m very fond of the craft but I have no desire to go into the industry. Wood lives and is something that ties us from the present back to the stone age, it’s a craft I’d save for ritual. Taking time to make something with your bare hands like this is the only way you can do the material justice. Rushing it is a pity that causes many mistakes.

I’m very limited in what I can say about programming.

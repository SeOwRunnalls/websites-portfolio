D&D

One of my favourite hobbies for the last few years was the TTRPG Dungeons and Dragons, I used to be a Game Master, meaning I wrote a story, a setting that served its purpose, designed compelling characters and plot threads to keep my players engaged. Since the start of my course I had to step away from it but I hope to return soon.

Playing guitar

I’ve always been infatuated with music but especially in my teens I started listening to metal, Iron Maiden mainly. from there Avatar, Amon Amarth, Arch Enemy, Alien weaponry, Opeth, Power Wolf. More recently I’ve been listening to a lot of Alex terrible, Slaughter to Prevail. Slipknot will always be one of my GOATS, speaking of if you’ve not heard of them look up polyphia. All of these bands have been great inspirations for me to pick up the instrument. For playing it, again, no time. But here and there I try to get a ‘Symphony of Destruction’ in.

Psychology

In my life psychology remains a topic close to my heart aside from my own struggles with my health I also find it an engaging topic. The inner workings of the mind fascinate me, and how we can be so unknowing of all the elements that shape and move us through the world.

Gaming

Gaming, where to start? I’ve been a PC gamer for the last few years. I started with the PlayStation 2 playing an obscure bubble game I can’t remember the name of, from there it was Crash Bandicoot, Spyro the Dragon. Followed up by Little Big Planet I loved those games as a kid played most of them. After that came the glory days of Call of Duty Modern Warfare 2, the original Black Ops. And then in 2011 Skyrim and since then it’s been slim pickings. Shareholders smelled money and the industry pivoted from passion to profit. And now we wait for the independent studios to deliver us from the drought of quality games.

Philosophy

I’m not attached to a certain school of thought or a particular thinker like Kant, Plato, Nietzsche, Voltaire and Epictetus. I’m more of an eclectic. I want to study the subject more, but who has the time. I like how when you look at something like the Greek concept of the ‘Logos’ and the Daoist ‘Dao’ and how those to concepts rhyme, yet these two had no effect on each other’s inception like.

Writing

I love to write, both by hand and in general, but I find that penmanship is calming. Also, it requires me to think, the pen doesn’t allow for mistakes at least not ones that can be hidden well. So, it forces the writer to think and take his time. Something we don’t do often and for which we are afforded very little space. Who cares about quality in a quantity world right, that thought and higher functions take a back seat, when has that had negative implications? But this interest took root at around the same time as my D&D antics started and was completely in service of that. I’ve been holding off on writing a book, a couple, actually. I’ve been told before that I’m a terrible writer and while on some level that may have something to do with it. It’s more so that I know my ideas to be out there and would be written for a niche audience cyphered away in the cracks on the fringes of polite common society. Bon, it is something I could do when all else fails.
